Transitioning from Part 19 to Subpart 58-6

An analysis of the proposed regulatory changes for New York State Clinical Laboratory Directors. This proposal aligns NYS requirements with CLIA and introduces a new Technical Director Certificate of Qualification.

Dashboard

A driver for this regulatory update is to harmonize New York State standards with CLIA requirements. This dashboard visualizes the scale of the impact and the core objectives of the transition from Part 19.

Goal

CLIA Compliance

Aligns with CLIA in order for NYS to secure it's status renewal in 2027.

Impact

~13% of current directors will need to transition to "Technical Director" roles or obtain certification.

Strategic Necessity

1

Maintain Exemption

NYS needs to remain the primary accrediting authority. Failure to update by 2027 puts this at risk.

2

Prevent Duplication

Avoids creating duplicative oversight programs (State + Federal) for NY laboratories.

3

Standardization

Ensures all directors meet the same high standard: Board Certification.

Detailed Comparison: Part 19 vs. Subpart 58-6

This interactive table outlines the specific differences between the existing Part 19 regulations and the proposed Subpart 58-6. The core shift is toward stricter qualification requirements for Directors, balancing this by formalizing the "Technical Director" role for those with technical expertise but lacking board certification.

Role Evolution: Director vs. Technical Director

The proposal creates a clearer distinction between the overall administrative/clinical lead (Laboratory Director) and the technical expert (Technical Director). This allows individuals who are highly skilled but lack board certification to continue serving in a vital technical capacity.

Implementation & Adverse Impact Analysis

Who is affected?

The changes apply to all regions of the State. Specifically, 85 out of 665 laboratories currently employ directors who do not meet the new board certification requirement.

Can the impact be minimized?

The report states there is no available mechanism to further minimize impact, as this is to align with CLIA requirements.

"Failure to make these revisions could result in the denial of that exemption in 2027, which would create duplicative laboratory oversight programs..."

Alternative for Affected Personnel

While they cannot serve as "Laboratory Director", these individuals may be eligible to serve as Technical Directors.
